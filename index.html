Bondi Sushi

Bondi, a fine-casual sushi restaurant, serves conscientious fish, cut clean, and served fresh. All of Bondi’s vendors are Certified B Corporations that meet rigorous standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. Bondi’s clean and minimal aesthetic, embodies their commitment to sustainability, and is also inspired by the several destinations where Bondi’s ingredients are sourced. Bondi was the sister project to Noda, and both projects were constructed simultaneously.

Noda

Noda is an 8 seat, high-end Omakase style sushi restaurant. Designed by the venerable Ken Fulk, the restaurant is characteristically Fulk: fantastical, luxe, and well-considered. Different from many high-end sushi restaurants, the Noda project veers from the typical minimal Japanese aesthetic and instead ventures into a dark, almost opium den inspired vibe. Noda was the sister project to Bondi, and both projects were constructed simultaneously.

Hedlund Residence

A uniquely hand-crafted home perched above downtown Santa Cruz on a cul de sac with views of Monterey Bay. Exotic hardwood details and wide plank reclaimed maple floors are showcased with accents of copper, tiles, and a variety of stonework. The hacienda style home was renovated to include a second floor, an accessory dwelling unit, and a subdivision of its oversize lot. Work included the in-fill of an existing pool, a foundation up renovation, a full radiant heating system, custom finishes throughout the home, solid wood millwork, and extensive exterior hardscaping.
